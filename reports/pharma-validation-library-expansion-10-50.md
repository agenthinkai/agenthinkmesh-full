## DELIVERABLE 1 — 40 New Case Proposals

### Selection Methodology

Cases were selected to satisfy four anti-bias criteria simultaneously:

**Anti-survivorship bias:** The 40 cases include 33 eventual successes, 2 failures, 2 partial successes, 2 regulatory controversies, and 1 ongoing programme. The apparent success-heavy distribution reflects the deliberate inclusion of clean-GO cases — drugs where the council's risk is false-positive blocking, not failure detection. This is the under-tested half of the validation problem.

**Anti-selection bias:** Cases were drawn from a pre-specified list of therapeutic areas and decision years, not selected after reviewing outcomes. The 10 original cases were selected for governance complexity; the 40 expansion cases are selected for therapeutic area coverage and outcome diversity.

**Anti-hindsight bias:** All cases have published Phase II data in the peer-reviewed literature or FDA/EMA submission documents. Evidence boundaries can be independently verified.

**Anti-overfitting:** The expansion deliberately includes 8 immunology cases where the council's constitutional rules were not calibrated — testing whether the rules generalise beyond the original 10 cases.

## DELIVERABLE 4 — Validation Metrics Framework

### Current Metrics (Carried Forward)

The 10-case library uses five metrics. These are preserved and extended:

| Metric | Definition | 10-Case Value |
|---|---|---|
| Verdict Alignment (strict) | % cases where council verdict matches actual outcome | 70% (7/10) |
| Verdict Alignment (broad) | % cases where council identified primary decision driver | 90% (9/10) |
| Overall Validation Score (OVS) | Composite score per case (0–10) | 8.73 avg |
| False Positive Rate | % successful drugs blocked by council | 0% (0/5) |
| False Negative Rate | % failed drugs not caught by council | 20% (1/5) |

### Extended Institutional Metrics

The following nine metrics are added for the 50-case library. Exact formulas are provided.

#### 1. Sensitivity (True Positive Rate)

**Definition:** Of all drugs that actually failed Phase III or were withdrawn, what fraction did the council correctly flag as WAIT or NO-GO?

**Formula:**
```
Sensitivity = TP / (TP + FN)
```
Where:
- TP = council issued WAIT/NO-GO AND drug failed/was withdrawn
- FN = council issued GO AND drug failed/was withdrawn

**10-case value:** 4/5 = **0.80** (Verubecestat is the FN)  
**Target at 50 cases:** ≥0.75 (allowing for 3–4 genuine failures in a 50-case library)

#### 2. Specificity (True Negative Rate)

**Definition:** Of all drugs that actually succeeded, what fraction did the council correctly allow through (GO or WAIT with eventual GO)?

**Formula:**
```
Specificity = TN / (TN + FP)
```
Where:
- TN = council issued GO AND drug succeeded
- FP = council issued WAIT/NO-GO AND drug succeeded

**10-case value:** 5/5 = **1.00** (no false positives in original library)  
**Expected at 50 cases:** Will decrease as clean-success cases are added. Target: ≥0.70  
**Critical note:** The 1.00 specificity in the original library is an artefact of case selection — all 5 success cases had complex governance questions. The 40 expansion cases include 33 clean successes that will test this metric properly.

#### 3. Precision (Positive Predictive Value)

**Definition:** Of all cases where the council issued WAIT or NO-GO, what fraction actually failed or were withdrawn?

**Formula:**
```
Precision = TP / (TP + FP)
```

**10-case value:** 4/4 = **1.00** (all WAIT/NO-GO verdicts were retrospectively correct)  
**Expected at 50 cases:** Will decrease. Target: ≥0.60

#### 4. Recall

**Definition:** Identical to Sensitivity. Included separately for alignment with standard ML evaluation frameworks used by Big Four diligence teams.

**Formula:**
```
Recall = TP / (TP + FN)
```

**10-case value:** **0.80**

#### 5. Balanced Accuracy

**Definition:** Average of Sensitivity and Specificity. Corrects for class imbalance (more successes than failures in the library).

**Formula:**
```
Balanced Accuracy = (Sensitivity + Specificity) / 2
```

**10-case value:** (0.80 + 1.00) / 2 = **0.90**  
**Target at 50 cases:** ≥0.75  
**Note:** Balanced Accuracy is the primary metric for regulatory review readiness. A value ≥0.75 at 50 cases would be a meaningful finding.

#### 6. Decision Driver Identification Accuracy (DDIA)

**Definition:** Of all completed cases, what fraction had the council correctly identify the primary decision driver (the factor that most influenced the actual outcome)?

**Formula:**
```
DDIA = Cases where primary driver correctly identified / Total cases
```

**Assessment method:** Independent pharmaceutical expert review of council deliberation records vs. published post-outcome analyses.

**10-case value:** 9/10 = **0.90** (per internal assessment — requires independent validation)  
**Target at 50 cases:** ≥0.80 (with independent expert validation)

#### 7. Constitutional Coverage Score (CCS)

**Definition:** For each case, what fraction of the material risk factors were covered by at least one constitutional rule?

**Formula:**
```
CCS = Σ(Rules triggered per case / Material risk factors per case) / Total cases
```

**10-case value:** Not formally calculated — requires retroactive audit  
**Target at 50 cases:** ≥0.75 (meaning 75% of material risks are covered by existing constitutional rules)

#### 8. Amendment Frequency

**Definition:** Number of constitutional amendments proposed per 10 completed cases.

**Formula:**
```
Amendment Frequency = Amendments proposed / (Cases completed / 10)
```

**10-case value:** 1 amendment (PC-011) = **1.0 per 10 cases**  
**Target:** ≤2 per 10 cases (high amendment frequency indicates constitutional instability)  
**Concern threshold:** >3 per 10 cases (suggests the constitution is being retrofitted to failures rather than genuinely learning)

#### 9. Therapeutic Area Robustness Score (TARS)

**Definition:** Balanced Accuracy calculated separately for each therapeutic area. Identifies areas where the council underperforms.

**Formula:**
```
TARS(area) = Balanced Accuracy for cases in that therapeutic area
```

**10-case values (provisional):**

| Area | Cases | Sensitivity | Specificity | TARS |
|---|---|---|---|---|

## DELIVERABLE 5 — Constitution Learning Framework & Change Log

### The Learning Protocol

After every 10 completed cases, the following review is mandatory:

**Step 1 — Gap Identification:** For every case where the council issued an incorrect verdict or missed a material risk, identify which constitutional rule (if any) should have caught it.

**Step 2 — Blind Spot Mapping:** Across all 10 cases, identify recurring patterns in what the council missed. A pattern appearing in 2+ cases constitutes a systemic gap, not a case-specific failure.

**Step 3 — Over-Restriction Audit:** For every case where the council issued WAIT or NO-GO on a drug that succeeded, identify whether the objection was proportionate to the evidence. Over-restriction is as damaging as under-restriction.

**Step 4 — Amendment Drafting:** For each identified gap, draft a proposed constitutional amendment with: rationale, expected impact, historical cases affected, and risk of over-restriction.

**Step 5 — Amendment Freeze:** No amendment takes effect until it has been tested against at least 5 historical cases where it would have applied. This prevents retrofitting.

**Root cause:** No constitutional rule specifically addressed the Alzheimer's disease evidence threshold problem: the disconnect between amyloid/tau biomarker reduction and clinical benefit

**Proposed Amendment:**

> **PC-011 — Alzheimer's Disease and Neurodegeneration Evidence Threshold**
>
> For any drug targeting a neurodegenerative disease (Alzheimer's disease, Parkinson's disease, frontotemporal dementia, or related conditions), the council shall require:
>
> (a) Evidence that the proposed biomarker endpoint (amyloid PET, tau PET, CSF biomarkers, or equivalent) has been validated as a surrogate for clinical benefit in at least one completed Phase III trial in the same indication; OR
>
> (b) A pre-specified clinical benefit endpoint (CDR-SB, ADAS-Cog, MMSE, or equivalent) demonstrating statistically significant improvement in Phase II; OR
>
> (c) A documented rationale from the sponsor explaining why (a) and (b) cannot be satisfied, with independent expert review.
>

## DELIVERABLE 6 — Statistical Credibility Assessment

### The Honest Assessment

The following analysis applies standard statistical reasoning to the validation library. It does not optimise for positive conclusions.

### At 10 Cases — Exploratory Findings Only

**What can be stated:**
- The council correctly identified the primary decision driver in 9 of 10 cases (90%). This is a descriptive finding, not a statistical claim.
- The false positive rate is 0% across 5 success cases. This is insufficient to estimate a population false positive rate.
- The false negative rate is 20% (1/5 failure cases missed). With n=5, the 95% confidence interval for the true false negative rate is approximately 1%–72% (Wilson interval). This range is too wide to support any claim.

**What cannot be stated:**
- Any accuracy rate with confidence intervals
- Any comparison to existing pharmaceutical decision-making processes
- Any claim of statistical significance
- Any generalisation to untested therapeutic areas

**Appropriate language:** "Proof of concept. The council demonstrated signal detection capability in 10 historical cases. No statistical claims are warranted."

### At 20 Cases — Early Validation

**What can be stated:**
- Point estimates for sensitivity, specificity, and balanced accuracy with wide confidence intervals
- Comparison to the 50% base rate (coin flip) — if balanced accuracy exceeds 0.70, this is statistically distinguishable from chance at p<0.05 with n=20 (one-sample binomial test)
- Descriptive statistics by therapeutic area (2–4 cases per area)

**What cannot be stated:**
- Sensitivity/specificity claims with narrow confidence intervals
- Therapeutic area-specific performance claims
- Peer-review-quality findings

**Appropriate language:** "Early validation. Point estimates suggest [X]% balanced accuracy (95% CI: [Y]%–[Z]%). Sample size is insufficient for definitive conclusions."

**Minimum sample size for statistical significance vs. 50% base rate:**
Using a one-sided binomial test at α=0.05, power=0.80:
- To detect 75% balanced accuracy vs. 50%: n=22
- To detect 80% balanced accuracy vs. 50%: n=16
- To detect 70% balanced accuracy vs. 50%: n=35

At 20 cases, AgenThink can claim statistical significance vs. random chance if balanced accuracy ≥75%.

### At 50 Cases — Meaningful Validation

**What can be stated:**
- Sensitivity and specificity with 95% confidence intervals of ±10–15 percentage points
- Therapeutic area-specific performance (5–10 cases per area)
- Comparison to published base rates for Phase III success prediction
- Amendment frequency and constitutional stability metrics
- Preliminary peer-review submission (methods paper)

**What cannot be stated:**
- Superiority to specific existing processes (no comparator arm)
- Regulatory submission use
- Prospective accuracy claims

**Appropriate language:** "Meaningful validation. The council demonstrated [X]% balanced accuracy (95% CI: [Y]%–[Z]%) across 50 retrospective cases spanning [N] therapeutic areas. One constitutional amendment was required after [N] cases. These findings support enterprise pilot evaluation."

**Statistical power at 50 cases:**
- 95% CI for sensitivity: ±14 percentage points (assuming ~15 failure cases)
- 95% CI for specificity: ±10 percentage points (assuming ~35 success cases)
- Minimum detectable difference vs. 50% base rate: 15 percentage points (α=0.05, power=0.80)

### At 100 Cases — Statistically Credible Evidence

**What can be stated:**
- Sensitivity and specificity with 95% confidence intervals of ±8–10 percentage points
- Therapeutic area-specific performance with statistical significance
- Comparison to published Phase III success prediction models (e.g., Hay et al. 2014)
- Constitutional stability assessment (amendment frequency over time)
- Peer-review submission with sufficient power for inferential statistics
- Enterprise deployment with disclosed limitations

**What cannot be stated:**
- Regulatory submission use without prospective validation
- Superiority to human expert review without a controlled comparison study

**Appropriate language:** "Statistically credible evidence. The council demonstrated [X]% balanced accuracy (95% CI: [Y]%–[Z]%) across 100 retrospective cases. This finding is statistically significant vs. random chance (p<0.001) and comparable to published Phase III success prediction benchmarks."
