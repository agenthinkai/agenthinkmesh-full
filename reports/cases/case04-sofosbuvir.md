SECTION B — DECISION BRIEF

DRUG NAME AND INN: Sofosbuvir (GS-7977)
SPONSOR: Gilead Sciences (acquired Pharmasset, original developer)
THERAPEUTIC AREA AND INDICATION: Hepatitis C virus (HCV) infection, across multiple genotypes.
MECHANISM OF ACTION: Sofosbuvir is a direct-acting antiviral that functions as a nucleotide analogue inhibitor. Specifically, it targets the Hepatitis C virus (HCV) NS5B RNA-dependent RNA polymerase. By mimicking a natural nucleotide, sofosbuvir is incorporated into the nascent viral RNA chain during replication, leading to premature chain termination and inhibition of viral replication. This mechanism is highly specific to HCV and offers a high barrier to resistance.
PHASE II PRIMARY RESULTS: Phase II trials demonstrated high sustained virologic response (SVR) rates. For instance, an all-oral combination of GS-7977 with daclatasvir showed SVR4 (SVR at 4 weeks post-treatment) in 100% of genotype 1 and 91% of genotype 2 and 3 treatment-naive patients. Another study involving GS-7977 plus ribavirin in genotype 1 treatment-naive patients reported 88% SVR4. These results were significantly higher than existing interferon-based therapies.
PHASE II SAFETY PROFILE: The safety profile of GS-7977 in Phase II trials was generally favorable. Adverse events (AEs) were typically mild to moderate, with fatigue, headache, and nausea being commonly reported. Discontinuation rates due to adverse events were low, especially when compared to interferon-containing regimens. No significant new safety concerns emerged from these early studies.
SAFETY SIGNALS: No major safety signals requiring mechanistic investigation were prominently reported in the available Phase II data. The drug appeared well-tolerated, with AEs consistent with those expected in HCV patient populations or related to co-administered drugs (e.g., ribavirin).
COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE: The HCV treatment landscape in 2012 was dominated by pegylated interferon and ribavirin, which had significant side effects and limited efficacy, particularly in certain genotypes. First-generation direct-acting antivirals like telaprevir and boceprevir had recently been approved but were used in combination with interferon and ribavirin, adding to treatment complexity and side effects. GS-7977 represented a potential paradigm shift towards interferon-free, all-oral regimens, offering a significant competitive advantage.
REGULATORY CONTEXT: The FDA and EMA were actively seeking more effective and tolerable treatments for HCV. While no specific breakthrough designation was in place for GS-7977 at this exact decision point, the high SVR rates and favorable safety profile positioned it as a strong candidate for an accelerated review pathway. The unmet medical need for interferon-free options was substantial.
FINANCIAL CONTEXT: Gilead Sciences acquired Pharmasset for approximately $11 billion in November 2011, primarily for its nucleotide analogue pipeline, with GS-7977 being the lead asset. This substantial investment underscored Gilead\'s strategic commitment to the HCV market and the perceived blockbuster potential of GS-7977. Development costs to date for GS-7977 were significant, reflecting the advanced stage of Phase II trials and the acquisition cost.
